The Seager Family Story

From a wartime roadside stall to a beloved Somerset farm shop

Seager's has been growing strawberries on Wells Road, Draycott, since Margaret Seagers first set up her roadside stall while her husband Ted served as a mechanic in the Second World War. What started as a small wartime venture grew into a beloved local business spanning three generations.

Margaret's son Andrew left school at 14 to help expand the farm, working alongside his mother to build the business. Today, Andrew's son Chris Seagers carries the tradition forward as the third generation, with Chris's son Lenny now helping out on the farm too.

We're proud to be a small, customer-friendly business serving the Cheddar Valley community. Our strawberries are handled only once — when they're picked — and sold the same day at our shop and at Bristol's daily food market.

Our Produce

Grown within metres of our stall — the freshest you'll find in Somerset

Strawberries

Our famous Cheddar Valley strawberries — picked by hand and handled only once. Grown in 125 metres of polytunnels with 29,000 plants. Around 100 trays picked daily in peak season.

Read more ▼

Our strawberries are grown in 125 metres of polytunnels on the south-facing slopes of the Mendip Hills, using bees for natural pollination. We have around 29,000 plants split between our greenhouse and polytunnels, producing fruit from late spring through summer.

Every strawberry is hand-picked and handled only once — at the moment of picking. That's what keeps them so fresh and undamaged. In peak season (May-June) we pick around 100 trays a day.

The Cheddar Valley has been famous for its strawberries since the 1800s, grown on the south-facing slopes of the Mendip Hills. Our family has been growing them here since the 1940s.

Seasonal Fruit & Veg

Top quality fruit and vegetables all year round. Also available at Bristol's daily food market.

What's in season? ▼

We stock a wide range of fresh fruit and vegetables all year round — apples, pears, potatoes, carrots, onions, cabbages, cauliflowers, broccoli, courgettes, tomatoes, cucumbers, lettuces, runner beans, peas, beetroot, plums, cherries, raspberries, blueberries, blackberries, rhubarb and more.

Our produce changes with the seasons, so there's always something fresh from local growers. We also sell at Bristol's daily food market and supply local shops and restaurants across the Cheddar Valley.

Pumpkins

Homegrown pumpkins available in autumn.

More about pumpkins ▼

In autumn, we grow our own pumpkins right here on the farm. Chris has been known to help the Draycott Community Shop pick the best ones for their window display!

We grow a variety of pumpkin types — from the classic big orange ones for carving to smaller cooking pumpkins. Available from late September through October (while stocks last).

Perfect for Halloween carving, roasting, soups, and pies. Pop by the farm shop on Wells Road to pick yours.

Christmas Trees

Fresh Christmas trees available during the festive season.

More about trees ▼

From late November, we sell fresh Christmas trees at the farm shop — available in small and large sizes to suit any home. All trees are fresh, locally sourced, and full of that classic festive scent.

Alongside trees, we stock seasonal festive produce including winter veg, sprouts, parsnips, and all the trimmings for your Christmas dinner.

Once the trees are gone, they're gone for the year — so come early to avoid missing out! We've been supplying Christmas trees to the Draycott community for years.

The Strawberry Line

The Cheddar Valley has been famous for strawberries since the 1800s, grown on the south-facing slopes of the Mendip Hills. The Cheddar Valley Railway — nicknamed "The Strawberry Line" — once carried millions of freshly picked strawberries to London markets.

Today, the old railway line is a 10-mile walking and cycling path from Cheddar to Yatton, with plans to extend from Shepton Mallet to Clevedon. And Seager's strawberries still grow in the very same soil they did a century ago.

"The locally grown strawberries still grow today in the very spots they did back then." — Strawberryfield Park
